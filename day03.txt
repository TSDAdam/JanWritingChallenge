Cone
----
Tear the shrinkwrap off.

My fingertips ease a small gap between the outer and inner boxes, sufficient only to allow air to flood the vacuum their parting will cause. The box lifts. I can feel the linen texture of the outer box beneath my fingertips as friction loses its futile struggle with gravity, and the boxes part ways.

New game smell.

The punchboards flex and protest while the tokens they support are popped with joyous satisfaction from their frames, my thumbs delighting in breaking their resistance. The perverse delight I take in the unmistakable noise they make feels like I'm doing something wrong, something taboo.

Unfold the board and whack it on the table.

The rulebook shares its secret wisdom and the game's setup follows like a religious ceremony. The Voynich-like mystery of the iconography and illustrations alluding to the theme of our shared collective escape from the real world, into that of an elaborate abstract fantasy.

Or maybe we're farming.

The level of concentration I need, broken only by the relative ease of the social interactions I would otherwise avoid like a coronavirus, means that my brain can rest. The exhausting, never-ending thrum of my internal monologue is laid to rest, if only for a precious couple of hours.

The person who lives closest to water starts.
