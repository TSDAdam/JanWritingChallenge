Rising, Falling

The steps down were worn, and slick with rotting leaves. These boots had seen it all though, and were more capable of keeping him from a fast, graceless descent. The smell from the gutter was foul, like rotting eggs. Eggs, golden yolks spilling and coating bread torn from a fresh cobbler. It had been a very long time since he'd eaten anything, let alone a warm, filling meal of eggs.

Light from the inn's windows coated everything it hit with a warm, welcoming glow, like so much honey. Light warmer than the dank November gloom that shrouded the land right now. He could almost feel the thawing relief of the open fire's hot, crackling licks. The venomous his and spitting sap from the young green logs were one thing. The comforting embrace of Prometheus' gift was another thing entirely.

Some fifty eyes turned their untrusting gaze upon him as he bundled in through the door. The innkeeper's skittled smile defied the frigid welcome and bade him closer to the bar. His knees protested mounting the bar stool, as if it were yet another horse on yet another road. The man sipped the tawny ale and he spoke. Cold, clawing words full of forboding.
